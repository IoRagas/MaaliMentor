# Inflation & Money Value (مہنگائی اور روپے کی قدر)

Inflation is the silent killer of wealth. Understanding how it works is vital to protecting your savings over time.

---

## 1. What is Inflation?
**Inflation (مہنگائی)** is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, and subsequently, purchasing power falls.

### Example of Purchasing Power Loss:
If a bag of flour costs PKR 1,000 today, and inflation is 20% this year, that same bag of flour will cost PKR 1,200 next year. Your PKR 1,000 note can no longer buy the whole bag; its purchasing power has eroded by 20%.

---

## 2. Inflation in Pakistan
Pakistan has historically experienced high, volatile inflation rates (often between 12% and 25%+). Keeping cash under the mattress or in 0% interest accounts guarantees that your money is losing value every single day.

---

## 3. Nominal Wealth vs. Real Purchasing Power
* **Nominal Wealth (نامیاتی دولت)**: The absolute face value of your money. (e.g., you have PKR 1,000,000).
* **Real Purchasing Power (حقیقی قوتِ خرید)**: What that money can actually buy, adjusted for inflation.
* **Calculation**:
  $$\text{Real Power} = \frac{\text{Nominal Wealth}}{\text{Cumulative Inflation Factor}}$$

---

## 4. How to Protection Your Wealth (Hedging)
To beat inflation, you must invest in assets that yield returns higher than the inflation rate:
* **Conventional Savings Accounts**: Usually yield (Inflation - 2%), which slows down loss but doesn't beat it.
* **Mutual Funds & Stocks**: Historically average 14-16% yield, beating inflation over long horizons.
* **Gold & Real Estate**: Tangible assets that naturally adjust in value as prices rise.
