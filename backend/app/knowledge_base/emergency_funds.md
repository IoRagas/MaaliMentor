# Emergency Funds & Debt Management (ایمرجنسی فنڈز اور قرض)

Building an emergency buffer and managing debt are critical steps to achieving financial security. These protect you from taking on high-interest debt when unexpected events happen.

---

## 1. What is an Emergency Fund?
An **Emergency Fund (ایمرجنسی فنڈ)** is money set aside to cover unexpected, essential expenses like:
* Medical emergencies.
* Job loss or business downturn.
* Major house or car repairs.

### How Much Do You Need?
 Pedagogy recommends saving **3 to 6 months of your basic living expenses** in an emergency fund. For example, if your monthly expenses are PKR 40,000, your target fund should be PKR 120,000 to PKR 240,000.

### Where to Store It?
Keep this money in safe, liquid accounts where you can access it within hours (e.g., Cash or a standard Bank Savings Account). Do not lock it in long-term investments.

---

## 2. Debt Management Basics
Debt can destroy financial stability if not managed properly.

### Types of Debt:
* **Good Debt**: Debt that builds long-term wealth (e.g., student loans, home mortgages).
* **Bad Debt**: Debt used for depreciating consumer products with high interest (e.g., Credit Card bills, consumer loans).

### local realities (Pakistan):
In Pakistan, credit cards and personal loans carry high interest rates (often KIBOR + 5% or more). High interest rates compound against you rapidly.

---

## 3. Debt Repayment Strategies
If you have outstanding debt, use these methods to clear it:
1. **Debt Snowball**: Pay off the smallest debt balances first for psychological wins, while paying minimums on others.
2. **Debt Avalanche**: Pay off the debt with the highest interest rate first to save the most money.
3. **Qard-e-Hasana (Islamic Loan)**: Seeking interest-free loans from family or Islamic charities to consolidate and pay off high-interest conventional debt.
