# Budgeting & Cash Flow Basics (بجٹ بنانا)

Budgeting is the foundational pillar of financial literacy. It is the process of creating a plan to spend your money, ensuring that you do not spend more than you earn, and allocating funds for future savings.

---

## 1. The Core Purpose of Budgeting
* **Income Tracking**: Knowing exactly how much money enters your household after taxes.
* **Expense Visibility**: Categorizing where your money goes to identify leaks.
* **Goal Alignment**: Directing money toward active goals (e.g., buying a bike, building emergency funds).

---

## 2. The 50-30-20 Rule
A simple, universally applicable guideline for budgeting is the **50-30-20 Rule**:
1. **50% Needs (زرویات)**: Vital living expenses you cannot avoid (e.g., house rent, electricity/gas bills, basic groceries, school fees).
2. **30% Wants (خواہشات)**: Lifestyle choices that improve comfort but are not essential (e.g., restaurant dining, shopping, movie tickets, travel).
3. **20% Savings (بچت)**: Direct savings and investments (e.g., emergency funds, mutual funds, gold).

---

## 3. Fixed vs. Variable Expenses
Understanding the difference helps in adjusting budgets when income changes:
* **Fixed Expenses (مستقل اخراجات)**: Stable costs that occur regularly (e.g., rent, utility bills).
* **Variable Expenses (غیر مستقل اخراجات)**: Costs that change monthly (e.g., entertainment, dining, medical emergencies).

---

## 4. Practical Tools for Budgeting in Pakistan
* **Diary Method**: Writing daily cash outflows in a small notepad (Parchi system).
* **Mobile Apps**: Using local digital wallets (like Easypaisa, JazzCash, or bank apps) and dedicated expense trackers.
* **Envelope System**: Allocating cash into physical envelopes for specific monthly categories.
